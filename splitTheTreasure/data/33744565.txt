                          <p class="introduction" id="story_continues_1" itemprop="description">    Substitute Rudy Gestede scored on his first competitive Aston Villa outing as Bournemouth were left to rue missed chances on their Premier League debut.
</p>
  
          <p>    Gestede, signed for £6m from Blackburn, powered in a header 13 minutes after replacing fellow new boy Jordan Ayew.
</p>
  
          <p>    In front of a packed and excited home crowd, Dan Gosling fired over from 12 yards, and Callum Wilson and Marc Pugh saw shots saved before the break.
</p>
  
          <p>    They were costly misses as Villa struck and comfortably defended the lead.
</p>

          <p>    Having come close to extinction as a club twice in their recent history, some Bournemouth fans will claim their side have seen more important days but there was tangible excitement at the Vitality Stadium as the club tasted Premier League football for the first time.

          <p>    Their tiny ground should have erupted in the opening period, most notably when Pugh wasted a glorious chance on the stroke of half-time.
</p>
  
          <p>    The winger smashed an effort into the ground from 12 yards, allowing Brad Guzan to save at a time which would have posed a lacklustre Villa serious questions.
</p>
  
          <p>    Instead, it is Eddie Howe left searching for answers. But the confidence with which his side played will give him encouragement - they held 58% of possession - ahead their next game at Anfield.
</p>
  
          <p>    More threat in the final third may be a desire, but the introduction of summer signing Max Gradel from the bench shows options are available.
</p>
  
    <h2 class="cross-head">Sherwood gets it right</h2>
    
          <p>    Tim Sherwood gave debuts to six summer signings as he embarks on his first full campaign in charge of a team.
</p>
  
          <p>    Perhaps his half-time words were behind the improvement as his side re-emerged quicker into the tackle and more threatening - notably when debutant Idrissa Gueye drew a fine save from Artur Boruc.
</p>
  
    <div class="story-feature image full-width">
  <img src="http://c.files.bbci.co.uk/127CE/production/_84762757_sherwood.jpg" width="624" height="260" alt="Tim Sherwood" itemprop="image" />

    <p class="caption" style="width:608px">Tim Sherwood has never started a season as a manager</span>
  </div>

          <p>    Sherwood has sought a presence up front to replace the 42 goals Christian Benteke contributed in his 89 league games, and in 6ft 4in Gestede, he has a player who contributed defensively from set pieces late on having already delivered the game&#039;s telling moment.
</p>
  
          <p>    This was far from a free-flowing, transformed Villa, but they stuck to their task doggedly and maybe things are turning as a goal from a substitute was a luxury which did not arrive once last season but took just 72 minutes in this campaign.
</p>
  
          <p>    Villa must improve on consecutive finishes of 16th, 15th, 15th and 17th if Sherwood is to back up his early promise as a manager but in making decisions at key times, he showed terrific instincts on the opening day.
</p>
  
    <h2 class="cross-head">Where Villa went toe-to-toe and won</h2>
    
    <div class="story-feature image full-width">
  <img src="http://c.files.bbci.co.uk/8F6C/production/_84761763_opta.jpg" width="624" height="260" alt="Opta Monitor" itemprop="image" />

    <p class="caption" style="width:608px">Bournemouth focused 49% of their attacks down Aston Villa&#039;s left</span>
  </div>

          <p>    With half a dozen players new to the league - including the disappointing Jordan Ayew - it was perhaps understandable Villa felt their way into the fixture.
</p>
  
          <p>    Howe&#039;s side were threatening from the right flank in the opening period but as Gueye and left-back Jordan Amavi grew into the game, Villa&#039;s left-side stiffened up.
</p>
  
          <p>    They were the most prominent away players on show, with Gueye&#039;s 74 touches only bettered by Amavi on 76 as the French full-back tackled ferociously and positioned himself well to ensure Villa won the battle down that key flank.
</p>
  
    <h2 class="cross-head">Manager reaction</h2>
    
          <p>    <strong>    Bournemouth manager Eddie Howe:

    &quot;I felt we dominated, especially possession and chances in the first half. If you&#039;re not clinical enough in their box and a player like Gestede comes on, you&#039;re in trouble. The chances will come at a premium this year and we need to take them.
</p>
  
          <p>    &quot;It was a great day for the football club regardless of the result. It&#039;s just a shame we couldn&#039;t back it up with the result, but I thought the performance was there.&quot;
</p>
