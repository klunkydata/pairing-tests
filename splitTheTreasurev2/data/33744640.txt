                          <p class="introduction" id="story_continues_1" itemprop="description">    Manchester United held on to start their season with a lacklustre win against Tottenham thanks to Kyle Walker&#039;s early own goal.
</p>
  
          <p>    After Christian Eriksen lobbed over when clean through, United went ahead as Walker poked into his own net when attempting to tackle Wayne Rooney.
</p>
  
          <p>    That led to a better spell for United, who did not have a shot on target until Ashley Young&#039;s 65th-minute effort.
</p>
  
          <p>    Sergio Romero - one of five United debutants - saved a late Eriksen drive. 
</p>

          <p>    Louis van Gaal celebrated his 64th birthday with a victory but his team were hanging on towards the end as Romero was twice called into action.
</p>
  
          <p>    Tottenham - who finished fifth in the Premier League last season, six points behind United - started and ended the game strongly only three days after completing their final pre-season game against AC Milan in Germany.

          <p>    Despite a gruelling summer in which he went on Spurs&#039; post-season tour to Australia and played in the England Under-21s&#039; ill-fated European Championship campaign, Kane looked sharp in the first half.
</p>
  
          <p>    He made a great start to the game, setting up Eriksen for his side&#039;s best chance after five minutes, but a lack of support meant Spurs&#039;s top scorer from last season faded and Chris Smalling began to boss the United defence in an impressive display. 
</p>
  
          <p>    But the 22-year-old did show promise after questions whether he could follow up last season&#039;s superb form. 
</p>
  
          <p>    Tottenham boss Mauricio Pochettino withdrew Nabil Bentaleb eight minutes into the second half after the 20-year-old lined up with Eric Dier in the centre of midfield and gave the ball away for the goal.
</p>
